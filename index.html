Caleb Kinney

Cybersecurity leader · Data storyteller · OSS builder · Dad

I cut my teeth on a hand-me-down Apple IIe, late-night IRC ops, and dial-up Doom deathmatches. That early hacker energy sparked a lifelong love of code. I taught myself HTML, CSS, and JavaScript by tweaking profiles on the ahead-of-its-time social network Arcadium.

By high school, I was hand-coding websites for the dot-com startup Iprose Internet · Unique Focus, building the first web presence for local businesses, and running LightOnline, an electronic-music e-zine. In college, I pivoted into design, crafting advertisements and magazine layouts for MWR Marketing in Yokosuka, Japan.

That indie-web chapter evolved into security work: from bug-bounty hunting to leading application-security and vulnerability-management programs. The same curiosity that had me reverse-engineering games now fuels how I blend hacker instincts with data-driven defense.

Today I’m Manager of Security Operations at Posit (formerly RStudio), shaping strategy that safeguards millions of data scientists and promoting a secure-by-design culture across product lines.

Open source keeps me grounded. I’m a core maintainer of Hacker Tracker, a DEF CON Goon, and an adviser to the NumFOCUS Security Committee. My security-focused code lives at github.com/TypeError, while data-driven experiments land at github.com/snally-labs (in active development).

Away from the keyboard you’ll find me logging miles on Maryland back roads, dialing-in craft pour-overs, sampling local beer, and adventuring with my wife and two imaginative daughters.
